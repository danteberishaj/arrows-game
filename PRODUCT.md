# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Casual mobile players on Android, playing in short relaxed sessions (commute, couch,
before bed). They want a calm but satisfying brain teaser — no timers, no pressure.
They open the app, tap Play, and expect to resume exactly where they left off.

## Product Purpose

"Arrows" is a minimalist logic puzzle: a grid of directional arrows; tap one to fire
it off the board in its direction. If the straight path to the edge is blocked by
another arrow, it's a collision (costs a heart). Clear the board to advance. The
puzzle is finding the order. Success = the player keeps flowing from one level to the
next without friction, and the game looks clean and premium — a faithful match to the
reference game (Arrows – Puzzle Escape), not a template.

## Brand Personality

Clean, calm, friendly. Three words: **bright, minimal, confident.** Gameplay should
feel effortless — the UI disappears into the puzzle: plain black arrows on white paper.
Identity is carried by a single friendly periwinkle accent and rounded type, not by glow
or noise.

## Anti-references

- Generic Unity-template look: chunky shapes on flat gray, default fonts, no craft.
- Busy hyper-casual ad-game UI: confetti spam, mismatched bright colors, fake buttons.
- Cluttered level-select grids and menus the player navigates every session.
- Dark / neon "glow" treatments — the reference game is bright white, not a neon theme.

## Design Principles

1. **The board is sacred.** Gameplay reads instantly — arrows are clear, evenly spaced,
   uniform in weight, never a chunky or inconsistent mess even on dense boards.
2. **One periwinkle accent.** Periwinkle-indigo carries identity (Play, level label,
   logo); coral-red is reserved for hearts and the blocked-arrow flash. Arrows are plain
   black on white. No decorative color soup.
3. **Flow over menus.** Open → Play → resume → next level. No level-select detour;
   progress is always saved.
4. **Motion conveys state.** In-game motion = feedback (exit slither, collision red-flash
   + shake, solved fade). The menu is calm and static — no ambient motion.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Text and arrows meet ≥4.5:1 (body) / ≥3:1 (large) against the white surface (black
  arrows ≈ 17:1; the large periwinkle Play label ≈ 4.6:1).
- Direction is conveyed by arrow *shape*, never color alone (colorblind-safe).
- Generous tap targets (full grid cell), portrait-only, large readable rounded type.
- Android can't expose prefers-reduced-motion without native plugins; in-game motion is
  brief feedback only and no gameplay action depends on animation.
